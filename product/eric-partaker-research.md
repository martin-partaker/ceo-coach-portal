## Core Frameworks

### 1. The IPA Framework
**Identity → Productivity → Antifragility**

The three pillars of his entire methodology:
- **Identity**: Who you are being, not just what you're doing. Behaviour change follows identity change.
- **Productivity**: Planning + Execution. "What gets scheduled gets done." Olympic Day Planner, weekly planning rituals.
- **Antifragility**: Resilience and courage under pressure. Doing hard things consistently.

### 2. The 3 Alarms
Three phone alarms tied to the three most important life domains:
- **Health** (e.g., 6:30 AM — "World Fitness Champion")
- **Wealth/Work** (e.g., 9:00 AM — "World's Best CEO")
- **Relationships** (e.g., 6:30 PM — "World's Best Husband and Father")

Each alarm is a named identity trigger — not a task reminder. You name the alarm as the version of yourself you want to embody.

### 3. The 3 Life Domains
All coaching and performance work is structured across:
- **Health** (physical and mental)
- **Wealth** (business/work)
- **Relationships** (family, team, partners)

The CEO Accelerator specifically focuses on the **Wealth** domain but acknowledges all three.

### 4. The Dream Team Exercise
Daily 5-minute reflection. For each life domain, answer:
1. **Identity** — "What is your identity in this domain? You at your best."
2. **Values** — Three words describing how this optimal version behaves
3. **Goal** — A compelling long-term aspiration for that domain
4. **Today's Highlight** — The single most important action proving alignment with your ideal self

### 5. Champion Proofs
One daily non-negotiable action per life domain that "proves" you are living as your best self. Small, consistent, evidence-generating.

### 6. 10X Goal Setter
A goal-setting approach that asks: "What would it take to achieve 10x your current result?" Forces non-linear thinking and constraint identification. Closely tied to the constraint-finding questions in coaching.

### 7. Identity → Behavior Cycle
```
Assumed identity → Aligned behaviors → Reinforced identity
```
The key insight: don't try to change behaviors directly — change the identity first, behaviors follow naturally.

## Coaching Assessment Questions (pre-session)

Eric uses structured assessments covering:

**Business/Company:**
- Is your company strategy clearly communicated to the team?
- Is your team composed of the right people in the right roles?
- Do you have clear cash flow projections?
- Is your sales process systematized?
- Have you clearly defined the core problem your product/service solves?
- Are your goals clearly defined and tracked?
- Is your product portfolio balanced?

**Personal/Leadership:**
- Are you stepping into difficult conversations when needed?
- Are you taking consistent action that generates results?
- Are you scheduling time for continuous learning?
- Do you have clarity about your business and life direction?
- Are you seeking constructive feedback from various sources?

## Key Principles for AI Prompt / System Context

1. **Progress over perfection** — celebrate movement, not just outcomes
2. **Constraints not problems** — frame obstacles as constraints to solve, not failures
3. **Commitment-based accountability** — what did you commit to, what happened, what next
4. **Identity first** — anchor feedback and goals to who the CEO is becoming, not just what they're doing
5. **Leverage** — always ask "what's the highest-leverage action here?"
6. **10x frame** — push for non-linear thinking, not incremental
7. **Sustainable performance** — high performance across health, wealth, and relationships
8. **Professional and reflective tone** — never diagnostic, never therapeutic, never prescriptive about mental health
